QUALIFICATION DESCRIPTION

I earned a certificate in Applied Mathematics from HarvardX, their online learning platform. Throughout the course, I tackled macro-economics projects, utilizing differential equations to estimate revenue trends. I also explored calculus-based approaches to analyze natural bifurcations in weather systems, such as the conditions that contribute to cyclone formation.

Additionally, I engaged in introductory quantum mechanics, where I applied Taylor expansions to approximate solutions in Einstein’s General Relativity. This program significantly enhanced my mathematical modeling skills and deepened my understanding of how complex systems behave across different scientific disciplines. You can find my certificate here.



WHY DID I CHOOSE IT?

Main reason was to learn how to make banging graphs and math models. Sceondary reason was because Applied Calculus is one of the most valuable courses an undergraduate can take, whether you're diving into engineering, economics, physics, or even data science. It’s not just about solving equations—it’s about understanding how things change and interact in the real world. From optimizing business profits to modeling climate change or designing better technology, calculus is the backbone of problem-solving.

A short course in Applied Calculus gives you the tools to think critically, analyze trends, and make data-driven decisions, all while sharpening your mathematical intuition. Plus, if you ever plan to go deeper into fields like AI, finance, or aerospace, having a solid calculus foundation puts you miles ahead.
